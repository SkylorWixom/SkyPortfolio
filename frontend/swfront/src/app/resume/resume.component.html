Career Journey

I started my professional career at Jiffy Lube when I was 16, washing windows and vacuuming cars. Jiffy Lube provided me with the flexibility to work through school as a non-traditional student. Along the way, I gained hands-on experience with vehicle maintenance and customer service, earning state inspection certifications. Within five years, I managed my own store and later became an assistant district manager, overseeing operations for five stores.

Jiffy Lube later expanded into automotive repair, and I earned ASE certifications to become a master mechanic. However, I wanted more, so I stepped down from management to focus on my education. I completed my bachelor's degree in December 2021, and in February 2022, I landed my first full-stack developer job.

My journey into software development began with Weber School District. I initially worked on an API that converted data from an outdated LANSA system to C# for exporting. Later, I tackled a nine-year ADA compliance audit, updating district and school websites to meet WCAG 2.1 standards.

Our team implemented a new student information system called PowerSchool, where I learned proprietary languages for custom reports, data manipulation, and state reporting. I also provided team training sessions, conducted code reviews, and gained invaluable experience collaborating in a team environment.

I'm now finishing my master's degree in computer science at Weber State University. My thesis explores fault reduction in C# using Hierarchical Delta Debugging and the Roslyn API to manipulate syntax trees. I'm seeking new opportunities to learn, grow, and make a meaningful contribution in the software development field.
